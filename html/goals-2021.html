Goals for 2021

Hey friends, the best way for starting a new year is by setting new goals and plans. A year is a long time, so an organized plan will definitely help you with your goals and perspectives. Obviously we are not machines, so it may happen that we change our vision about something and we abandon it. The important thing is to not have any regret next December! My goals for this year are divided in three categories: personal, academic and financial.

Personal

  • Read at least 36 books : last year I planned to read around 1 book per week, but I realized that sometimes I was just pursuing my goal without learning or enjoying that much. So, this year I introduced a “bonus week” per month, and let’s see what happens. In addition, last month I started listening to audiobooks in my down time and I really enjoy it, it is pleasant enough.

  • Write at least 24 articles on this blog : I would like to post at least 2 articles per month here. Actually I am enjoying writing down my thoughts, discoveries, learning tools and stuff like that, so I think I will keep it up for a while!

  • Plant three trees : well, it seems a bit weird but it is also really important. Me and my girlfriend decided to avoid buying useless gifts for Christmas and we planted a huge Baobab instead. We are lucky that we do not have any needs at the moment, so we tried to do something meaningful for the environment. If you are curious, here’s my buddy.

  • Make a trip : I have been dreaming to visit the Netherlands for a while and I hope that this year I will have the possibility to go there, but let’s see if the covid-19 will allow that.



Academic

  • Github : I have been using github for a couple of years but just for small projects and I have just tasted push, pull and merge. So I cannot say that I know Github. I have no excuses this year, I need to take some nice stuff to read and test them.

  • Financial ML moels : as you’ve seen, I am really keen into Finance and Investments, so I would like to combine it with my passion for the Artificial Intelligence world. I have some ideas and I will start studying some interesting modules after my winter exams. I don’t think I will study Algorithmic Trading Strategies because without a pure strategy to automate, it is a Russian Roulette and I don’t like to lose my money. Once I learn my trading strategy, I will try to automatize but It is not a priority for now, probably next year.

  • Streamlit : it is a python framework for creating interactive web applications for Data Science and Artificial intelligence. I saw a couple of videos on YouTube (just for curiosity) and it seems quite interesting.



Financial

This year I will think about long-term investments and once I have all ready I can start studying something a bit more aggressive. I already know which courses and books to buy, but they are not so cheap, so if I buy them I will not have the possibility to invest these money and test the strategy, so I need to wait a bit. The long-term investments that I mentioned previously are :

  • PAC (Savings Account) : PAC states for Piano di Accumulo Capitale” and I didn’t find any English equivalent (the closest one was “savings account”), but it is nothing but a long term investment based on the dollar-cost-averaging strategy. I started my 15-year “PAC” last November and I will invest € 250 per month until I get a job, then I will rise up to € 500. At the moment I am investing my savings and my previous scholarships, that thanks to my frugality, they are still in my bank account ahah

  • Retirement fund : another (extremely) long investment that I want to start this year, is my private retirement fund. In Italy the public retirement salaries are decreasing and decreasing and all the predictions for the future ones are catastrophic, so I want to be prepared.
