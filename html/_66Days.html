#66DaysOfData

Hey all!

It’s been a while since my last blog post (if anyone has noticed). Today I would like to talk about Ken Jee’s challenge. He is a data scientist and a youtuber, and he constantly gives some good tips for aspiring data scientists.

This challenge is called “66 Days of Data”. The goal is to learn something new (about data science, in this case) every day for at least 66 days. It is not required to do exhausting sessions every day, but the minimum amount of time should be around 5 minutes.

It may sound silly or useless, but they are not casual numbers. According to James Clear (the author of Atomic Habits), in order to maintain a new habit, we should practise for at least 5 minutes, for around 66 days.

That’s the trick! It is important to discover new things for the entire period, but it is even better to build up a habit that will last for a long time. I was planning to start it for a while, but the winter exams prevailed. So, once I’ll finish my last exam, I will finally start this challenge.

My plan for these 66 days is to improve my machine learning skills applied to the Finance sector. In particular I will start this journey with Marcos Lopez De Prado’s book: Advances in Financial Machine Learning. It was suggested to me by many people and it seems very interesting.

Then, another interesting part is that I won’t be alone, in fact I found two other guys to share this journey. We had some great ideas :D In my opinion it is something more than just a simple challenge, I suggest it to anyone, for any particular skill that you want to leverage!

See you soon,
